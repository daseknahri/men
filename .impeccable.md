## Design Context

### Users
- Public side: restaurant customers, primarily on mobile, moving quickly between discovery, menu browsing, and ordering.
- Admin side: a single restaurant owner or operator who mainly edits menu structure and a small amount of business identity information.
- Seller side: internal setup work should stay hidden or minimized in the shipped client experience.

### Use Cases
- Customers should understand the restaurant, reach the menu fast, switch language quickly, and place an order with minimal friction.
- Owners should edit menu structure, prices, item images, hours, WiFi, and branding without navigating a cluttered dashboard.
- The product should feel dependable enough to sell as a white-label restaurant web app with minimal custom code per client.

### Brand Personality
- Clean
- Mobile-first
- Warm

The experience should feel like a polished restaurant app rather than a generic admin dashboard or template landing page. It should feel calm, premium, and easy to operate.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Light, warm surfaces with tinted neutrals instead of stark white.
- High-contrast, app-like chrome on top of soft content sheets.
- Compact, touch-friendly controls.
- Clear hierarchy with minimal explanatory copy and reduced UI clutter.
- Motion should be restrained, smooth, and purposeful.

### Anti-Patterns To Avoid
- Desktop navigation patterns forced onto mobile.
- Repetitive helper text and duplicated labels.
- Card-inside-card UI clutter.
- Overly large empty spaces that slow task completion.
- Generic AI-looking gradients, glass effects, or decorative clutter without purpose.

### Quality Bar
- Sellable white-label product.
- Mobile UX is the highest priority.
- Prefer fewer, clearer controls over more functionality on screen at once.
